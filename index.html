Instructions for Use

Getting started with MouseKey

Basic Operation

Launch MouseKey, select a Profile, and assign an action from the dropdown for each click pattern (2, 3, 4, or 5 quick middle button clicks). Use the Enabled checkbox to turn each click pattern on or off. MouseKey waits briefly after your clicks to confirm the final count before executing the selected action.

Creating a Custom Hotkey or Macro

Select "Create Hot Keyâ€¦" from any dropdown to record keyboard shortcuts (like Ctrl+C or Alt+Tab) or text strings to be replayed later. Once saved, the custom action becomes available within the current profile.

Turning MouseKey ON or OFF

MouseKey has a master enable/disable state which can be activated from the main window or by holding the middle mouse button for 3 seconds. When disabled, middle mouse click behavior returns to normal. Re-enable at any time from the system tray icon menu.

System Tray & Background

MouseKey continues running when the main window is closed. The tray icon may be under the Windows "hidden icons" arrow. Right-click the icon to open the app, enable or disable MouseKey, or exit completely. Settings are automatically saved and restored on next launch.

Security Best Practice

When creating custom hotkeys or macros, do not record passwords, PINs, authentication codes, recovery keys, or other sensitive information. Recorded macros replay input exactly as captured. MouseKey does not analyze, transmit, or share recorded input.
