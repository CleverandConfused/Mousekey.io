MouseKey Pro Troubleshooting

Last updated: June 2026

MouseKey Pro is a one-time purchase ($6.99 USD, pricing varies by region). Most issues are related to the Microsoft Store, not MouseKey itself.

1. You don't have a Microsoft Account

The Microsoft Store requires a Microsoft Account for purchases. Create a free one at account.microsoft.com using any email address. Once created, sign into the Store app on your PC and purchase MouseKey Pro.

2. Work or school account can't purchase

Many organizations restrict Store purchases on managed accounts. You can add a personal Microsoft Account to the Store without changing your Windows login.

Open the Microsoft Store app, click the profile icon (top right), click Add account, and sign in with a personal account. Your Windows login stays the same. Purchase MouseKey Pro using the personal account.

3. Your organization blocks the Microsoft Store

Some IT departments disable the Store entirely via group policy. If you can't open the Store or see no results, you won't be able to purchase through it on that device.

MouseKey is also available as a portable version with a direct purchase option for Pro. No Microsoft Account or Store access needed.

4. Purchased but still shows Free

Restart MouseKey. The license check runs on startup. Right click the tray icon, select Exit, then reopen the app.

Check your account. Make sure you're signed into the Store with the same Microsoft Account you purchased with.

Different PC? The license is tied to your account, not the device. Sign into the Store with the same account and it should transfer automatically. Check Library for updates if it doesn't appear right away.
