MouseKey Pro Troubleshooting

Last updated: April 2026

MouseKey Pro is a one-time purchase of $6.99 (USD) through the Microsoft Store. Pricing may vary by region. If you're having trouble purchasing or activating it, this page covers the most common issues and how to resolve them.

1. You don't have a Microsoft Account

You need a Microsoft Account to purchase anything from the Microsoft Store. If you don't have one, you can create one for free in a few minutes.

How to create one:

1. Go to account.microsoft.com and click Create a Microsoft Account.

2. You can sign up with any email address you already have (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.) or create a new Outlook email during signup.

3. Follow the prompts to set a password and verify your email.

4. Once your account is created, open the Microsoft Store app on your PC. Click the profile icon in the top right and sign in with your new account.

5. Search for MouseKey Pro or open it from within the MouseKey app, and complete your purchase.

2. You have a work or school account but can't purchase

Many organizations restrict what their managed accounts can do in the Microsoft Store. If your work or school account won't let you complete a purchase, you don't need to change your Windows login. You just need to add a personal Microsoft Account to the Store app separately.

How to add a personal account to the Store:

1. Open the Microsoft Store app.

2. Click the profile icon in the top right corner.

3. Click Add account.

4. Sign in with a personal Microsoft Account. If you don't have one, see the section above to create one for free.

5. Once signed in, the Store will use your personal account for purchases. Your Windows login stays the same. Nothing changes about how you sign into your PC or your organization's apps.

6. Search for MouseKey Pro and complete your purchase using the personal account.

3. Your organization blocks the Microsoft Store entirely

Some IT departments disable the Microsoft Store through group policy. If this is the case, the Store app may not open at all, or it may open but show no results. You won't be able to install or purchase through the Store on that device.

If this applies to you, MouseKey is also available as a portable version that runs without installation. You can download it directly from mousekey.site without going through the Microsoft Store. The portable version includes a direct purchase option for Pro that doesn't require a Microsoft Account or Store access.

4. Purchase went through but the app still shows Free

MouseKey checks your license when it starts up. If you purchased Pro but the app still shows the free version, try these steps.

Restart MouseKey. Close the app completely (right click the tray icon and select Exit), then reopen it. The license check runs on startup, so a restart is usually all it takes.

Check your Microsoft Account. Make sure you're signed into Windows (or the Microsoft Store app) with the same Microsoft Account you used to make the purchase. If you used a different account, the Store won't recognize the license.

Purchased on a different PC? Your license is tied to your Microsoft Account, not to a specific device. As long as you're signed into the Store with the same account on your current PC, the license should transfer automatically. If it doesn't show up right away, open the Microsoft Store, go to Library, and check for updates.

Still need help?

If none of the steps above resolved your issue, submit a support request with a description of the problem and we'll help you get it sorted out.
